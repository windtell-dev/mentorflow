# Vision & Philosophy

## The one-liner

**TeachPath is a private teaching journal with intelligent structure.** You reflect for two minutes after a session; Gradient AI turns that into durable memory about how your learner learns.

## The problem

Millions of people teach one-on-one without any support structure: volunteer tutors, mentors, after-school staff, older siblings helping with homework. They have no lesson-prep time, no training in learning science, and no memory system — every insight about a learner lives in their head and evaporates.

Meanwhile, the tools that exist are built for institutions (Canvas), for content delivery (ChatGPT), or for gamified self-study (Duolingo). Nothing serves **the human relationship at the center of learning**.

## The stance

- AI **augments** the tutor; it never talks to the learner and never replaces the relationship.
- The tutor's observation is the primary data. AI's job is to remember, connect, and suggest — not to teach.
- One learner at a time. No cohorts, no class averages, no leaderboards.
- The most valuable feature is the one that takes the least time: a two-minute reflection that compounds into a real understanding of the learner.

## Differs from

- **Canvas / LMS** — institutional plumbing; TeachPath is personal practice
- **ChatGPT** — a blank box with no memory of your learner; TeachPath is structured memory
- **Lesson-plan generators** — generic worksheets; TeachPath plans from *this* learner's history
